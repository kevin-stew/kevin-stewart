MY DEVELOPER JOURNEY
I started learning to code and web-development skills in the late-pandemic era as a way to challenge myself and automate some of the most tedious tasks I found myself doing at work. Eventually I enrolled in a coding bootcamp to accelerate the pace of learning and rapidly expand my horizons. After successfully completing that training, I have continued to work on web-apps while also gaining experience in new languages and frameworks. I enjoy thinking about how to improve efficiencies in any process, how aspects of architectural design can be automated with web technologies, and turning vision into reality.

WHY TECH?
In a word: growth. The growth and opportunity available in the tech sector are unrivaled to any other, and with some effort and continued learning it is very accessible. Also, and importantly, I find it very interesting. Because technology has become so integrated into daily life, its influence has an outsized effect on our lived experiences. In the same way I aspired to improve the world through better spatial design, I believe even more potential exists to improve the world, and our day-to-day experiences, through better technology. At the onset of Web 3.0 and a wave of industry disrupting innovations, there is perhaps no better time to join this change and contribute to it.

LANDSCAPE ARCHITECTURE EXPERIENCE
I have spent the majority of my career (nearly a decade) working as a Landscape Architect; first as a designer, then credentialed professional, and finally as a project manager. During that time I had the chance to work on nearly every project type imaginable while gaining valuable skills that are highly transferable. I have project management experience and have worked in both domestic and international settings; the projects I oversaw were complicated and required the coordination of many disciplines with overlapping scopes.
